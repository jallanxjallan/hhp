  1. Visionary Beginnings

In 1989, a government deregulation package set the stage for increased foreign investment. This created a demand for international-standard legal services.

At the time, most Indonesian law firms were built around dominant personalities. Tuti Hadiputranto, who had been an associate in several such firms, realized that foreign investors planning long-term commitments preferred legal institutions over individual lawyers. Tuti’s vision of a corporate law firm greater than the sum of its parts led to the establishment of HHP.

HHP began modestly, with an office barely better than a shop-house floor. Nevertheless, the firm quickly rose to prominence in the Jakarta legal landscape, becoming the first local firm to “go international” by joining the Baker McKenzie global alliance—a network of independent law firms specializing in cross-border transactions and dispute resolution.

4. Navigating the Asian Financial Crisis

In late 1998, a meeting in an upmarket hotel nearly erupted into physical violence when an client negotiator told the CEO of an insolvent Jakarta taxi company that bankruptcy was the only viable option.** [Note: Please refer to the comment and recording for the story account, and assess whether it is suitable as a reference for this situation.] Over the following years, similar—if less dramatic—encounters occurred as once-thriving companies faltered under the weight of the Asian Financial Crisis.

But a substantial number of cases had more positive outcomes, as HHP skillfully employed its deep expertise in international finance and negotiation to restructure crushing debts. As chief legal advisor to IBRA, HHP played a critical role in restoring confidence in Indonesia’s banking sector during the economic recovery.

Through strategic adaptation, HHP—like many well-managed, internationally oriented clients—not only weathered the crisis but emerged stronger. At the turn of the millennium, the firm moved into Jakarta’s most prestigious commercial address: the Stock Exchange Building.

7. Changing of the Guard

In 2015, HHP cemented its position as Indonesia’s leading international corporate law firm by successfully restructuring the Berlian Laju tanker fleet. The outcome hinged on the trust stakeholders placed in the firm.

Yet this high-water mark was also a turning point. As the founding partners neared retirement, the firm’s commitment to institutionalization faced its first real test.

9. Modernization and Technological Adaptation

In 2015, three decades after the Telkom IPO secured HHP’s place in the top ranks of the legal profession, the spin-off of Indihome marked the firm’s entry into the digital services era and earned it the ALB M&A Deal of the Year award.

The firm then relocated to the Pacific Century building, Jakarta’s most technologically advanced commercial space. But the gleaming new offices stood mostly empty as the COVID-19 pandemic unfolded. HHP used this time to rethink legal service delivery, embracing remote work and streamlining operations to emerge leaner, more agile, and more efficient.
